[ ABOUT ]

The Person Behind the Foundation

Navaneet S V is a Computer Science Engineering student at Thiagarajar College of Engineering, Madurai, whose work sits at the intersection of sport, technology, and civic leadership. He believes that the same discipline that builds a great cricket team — clear roles, fair systems, and trust earned over time — can be applied to building stronger communities.

As the Founder of Cric Connect, he has built a platform and community for cricket enthusiasts to learn, connect, and grow. As the Organizer and Licensee of TEDxThiagarajar College of Engineering, he brings independently curated ideas worth spreading to his campus and city. He is also ICC Pitch Curator Foundation Course Certified, completing the program with a 95% score, and has had the opportunity to interview Australian cricket legend Brad Hogg.

Across every initiative, his focus remains the same: using cricket, technology, and education as tools for community impact and youth leadership.

[ MISSION & VISION ]

Why The Foundation Exists

Mission

To empower young people through cricket, technology, and education — creating platforms where talent is discovered, ideas are shared, and leadership is practiced, not just taught.

Vision

A community where every student-athlete and young innovator has access to the mentorship, tools, and stage required to turn potential into meaningful, lasting impact.

Core Values

01

Integrity

Leading with honesty in every pitch, partnership, and project, on the field and off it.

02

Innovation

Treating technology as a tool for fairness and access, not novelty for its own sake.

03

Service

Measuring success by the communities strengthened, not just the milestones reached.

04

Excellence

Holding every initiative, from a curated pitch to a curated idea, to a professional standard.

[ IMPACT AREAS ]

Where the Work Happens

Cricket Development

Growing the sport from the ground up — pitch science, player platforms, and access to expert knowledge for the next generation of cricketers.

Technology Innovation

Applying AI and software to age-old sporting problems, from umpiring decisions to civic service delivery.

Education

Curating ideas worth spreading through TEDxTCE and certified learning, including the ICC Pitch Curator Foundation Course.

Youth Leadership

Putting students in charge of real organizations and real events, so leadership is learned by doing, not by lecture.

Community Service

Building outreach programs that connect students, alumni, and local communities around shared civic goals.

[ FOUNDATION INITIATIVES ]

Social Impact, By Design

The Navaneet Foundation channels every project, partnership, and platform toward three standing initiatives. Each is built to outlast any single event or academic year.

I

Social Impact Projects

Long-term programs such as Cric Connect and civic technology efforts like GiftMoi, designed to solve real problems for real communities rather than serve as one-off showcases.

II

Youth Empowerment Programs

Structured opportunities, from TEDxTCE speaker mentorship to cricket talent pathways, that hand students real responsibility and real platforms early.

III

Community Outreach

Direct engagement with local schools, clubs, and cricket grounds, bringing certified knowledge and technology access to people who might not otherwise reach it.
